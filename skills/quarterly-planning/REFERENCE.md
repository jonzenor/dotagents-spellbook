# Quarterly Planning — Reference

## Brainstorm Conversation Guidelines

This is a back-and-forth conversation, not an interview. Work through life areas one at a time in whatever order feels most natural given the findings from Step 2 — start with the area that surfaced the most friction or drift.

For each area:
1. Share what you observed from the vault data (specific, not generic)
2. Ask **one focused question** — what does Jon actually want, not what he "should" do
3. Help him move from vague intention to outcome language: *"have X done"* not *"work on X"*
4. Probe gently when a goal sounds like obligation or avoidance rather than genuine intent

### Life Areas

| Area | Notes |

| **FOTF** | Day job at Focus on the Family. Keep strictly separate from Lighthouse. |
| **Lighthouse** | Side project Jon leads. Separate from FOTF — don't conflate. |
| **Personal / Home** | Home projects, finances, routines, life admin |
| **Spiritual / Faith** | Prayer, Bible study, church involvement, spiritual growth |
| **Health** | Physical, sleep, exercise, nutrition |
| **Relationships / People** | Family, friends, mentoring, community |

### Working Genius Awareness

Jon's frustration types are **Wonder** and **Tenacity**.

When a goal is Tenacity-heavy (requires sustained follow-through against resistance), name it explicitly:
> *"This one will require pushing through resistance — is that a real commitment for this quarter, or is this more aspirational?"*

Do not minimize the cost. Help Jon decide with eyes open. A goal he commits to knowing the cost is more likely to happen than one he commits to while ignoring it.

## Goal Format

Each goal should be:
- **Outcome language**: *"Launch the new onboarding flow"* not *"Work on onboarding"*
- **Bounded**: completable within the quarter or has a clear first milestone
- **Labeled**: life area + Tenacity flag if applicable

```
- [ ] [Goal statement] — milestone by [Month] [⚡ Tenacity]
```

Aim for 3–5 total goals. More than 5 is usually optimism, not planning.
